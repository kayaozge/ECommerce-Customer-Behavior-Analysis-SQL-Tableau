# ECommerce-Customer-Behavior-Analysis-SQL-Tableau
Customer behavior analysis using SQL and Tableau — transaction data, customer spend and product performance

## 📊 Project Overview
Global e-commerce customer behavior analysis covering December 2010 – December 2011.
Raw transaction data was cleaned, analyzed with SQL and visualized in Tableau.

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- **SQL / PostgreSQL** — data cleaning, customer spend, product and country analysis
- **Google Sheets** — data preparation
- **Tableau** — interactive sales dashboard

## 📈 Key Metrics
- Total Revenue, Total Orders, Average Order Value (AOV)
- Top 10 best-selling products
- Sales by country and month
- Customer segmentation (high-value vs occasional buyers)

## 🔍 Key Findings
- Total Revenue: £10,279,398 | Total Orders: 19,789
- Revenue peaks in November–December, driven by Black Friday and year-end campaigns
- Top 3 products (Regency Cakestand 3 Tier, Paper Craft Little Birdie, White Hanging Heart) account for a disproportionately high share of total revenue
- UK and Western Europe dominate sales geographically
- Q4 revenue significantly exceeds the annual monthly average
- Decorative items and gift sets are the primary sales drivers
- High-value customers are most active during campaign periods

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations
- Strengthen loyalty programs in the UK market
- Plan stock management from October to prepare for Q4 demand surge
- Run targeted campaigns for high-margin gift sets (JAM MAKING SET, ALARM CLOCK)
